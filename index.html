Getting Started

Before a new month starts, plan what your month's activites and expenses. Example if you're going on a road trip or have a doctor's appointment within the month, but don't move to the next.

If this is your first time budgeting, forecast how much money will be coming in for that month. The more realistic your numbers, you'll more likely stick to it. Also give yourself some grace, you'll get better with time.

Have different categories that will determine how you'll spend your money. Create rules that you would follow to help you be in line with your money. More will be touched in the next tip

Fill that bank account

These categories will help with what is considered a "need", a "want" and worth "saving" with your money. A need is 50% (Housing, Groceries, Bills), a want is 30% (Hobbies, Entertainment, Trips) and a savings is 20% (Retirement, Long-term goals).

Besides your savings and checking account, open up a 3rd or 4th account with your financial institution. It can be your vacation or 401K savings, not only is it great for interest rates but it will help discipline you to not spend any money from your savings.

Living life sometimes life happens, from a possible accident, getting let go from a job, or anything that is considered an emergency. It's always good to set extra money aside in case life gives you a "OOPS" moment.

Budget Calendar

Create specific dates for your expenses, paycheck, color code and set them as alerts. It also wouldn't hurt signing up for Autopay for your other bills. Not only will it keep you on track, elimate debts but some companies offer discounts!

Review your calendar each time you have a paycheck coming, this helps you plan where your money will go and make adjustments if needed. Example when your paying off a debt rather it be credit card or student loans and you decided to pay a little extra to pay it off. Remember each month is different, with different goals.

Tracking Your Budget

Nothing wrong of visually seeing how and where you're spending your money. There's free apps like Mint (yes they have ads and that can be a bit). Your financial institution may offer a visual of how you're spending your money through their mobile banking app.

At times we can go crazy with our spending and next thing you know your accounts aren't looking as planned. Don't be too hard on yourself it happens to all of us just be ready to adapt. Instead of ordering out, cook lunch/dinner instead, if you spend money on Uber/Lyft try your metro transit instead. Strategize on cutting your expenses to help bring everything back to balance.

Goals, Goals GOALS!

Set goals, start small then work towards a bigger goal. Short-term goals like savings for vacations or to have a certain amount in one of your savings are stepping stones for long-term goals like paying off mortgage to have savings in your IRA or 401K. Always remember what is your "why" for setting these goals for yourself.

While setting these goals, make sure you have a certain timeframe that is within the year. Rather it's 3, 6 months for a vacation or to have a certain amount in your savings or another account by the end of the year.

Don't compare your money situation to others, everyone's situation are different. Comparisons will only knock you off your track. Keep your eyes on the prize and give yourself some grace because slip ups are going to happen. Always remember this is a marathon and not a sprint and you're doing a good job for you and your family.
